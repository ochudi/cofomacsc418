Professor Enase Okonedo is an accomplished academic and leader in the field of management education. Currently serving as the Vice-Chancellor of Pan-Atlantic University in Lagos, Nigeria, she previously held positions such as Deputy Vice-Chancellor and Dean of Lagos Business School (LBS). Throughout her tenure at LBS, she played a pivotal role in its transformation into a globally recognized institution, securing accreditations and rankings that elevated its status in Africa.

Prof. Okonedo is recognized internationally for her contributions to management education, serving on the boards of organizations such as the Global Business School Network (GBSN) and the United Nations Principles for Responsible Management Education (UNPRME). She has also held leadership positions in prestigious organizations like the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC) and AACSB International.

Beyond academia, Prof. Okonedo is actively involved in Nigeria's business community, serving as an independent non-executive director on multiple boards. She is committed to promoting education in Africa through her role as President of the AIFA Reading Society and as a member of the Panel of Advisors for the Africa Initiative for Governance (AIG).

With a diverse educational background including a doctorate from the International School of Management (ISM), Paris, an MBA from IESE Business School, Barcelona, and a Bachelor's degree in accounting from the University of Benin, Prof. Okonedo brings a wealth of knowledge and expertise to her roles. She is recognized as a fellow of several prestigious institutes and is a prolific author and public speaker, addressing issues of management education and decision-making in emerging markets.
